# TikTok Template

Use this when turning a scene into a short video or social breakdown.

## Core structure

1. Hook
2. Context
3. Clip or subtitle moment
4. One phrase or one cultural point
5. Rating, punchline, or archive verdict if it helps
6. CTA

## Working script shape

- Hook: "Why does this random American scene still live in Cantonese-American memory?"
- Context: name the work, year, and what kind of scene it is.
- Clip moment: show the line, cadence, or visual clue that matters.
- Cultural point: explain one thing only. Do not cram the whole archive entry into the short.
- Punchline or verdict: if the scene is funny, let it stay funny.
- CTA: ask viewers to nominate the next culturally iconic American scene.

## Packaging reminders

- Use a cover title that can stand alone without the whole caption.
- Keep the short caption punchy.
- Put the deeper archive argument in the long caption or linked site entry.
- If the language call is uncertain, say that in plain English.

## Sample CTA lines

- "Nominate the next scene that belongs in the archive."
- "If you know this clip, send the timestamp."
- "If you can translate or verify it, the archive wants your help."
