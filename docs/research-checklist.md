# Research Checklist

Use this before moving any scene toward `ready-to-film` or `ready-to-publish`.

## Core verification

- Verify work title.
- Verify release year or air year.
- Verify episode title and episode number if applicable.
- Confirm creator, director, network, or studio where relevant.
- Confirm location or public setting if the scene depends on place.

## Language review

- Identify the language or dialect as specifically as possible.
- Note if the scene mixes Cantonese with Mandarin, English, or another variety.
- Mark `Unclear audio` if the hearing is rough.
- Mark community transcript work as community-sourced until verified.
- Add transcript confidence per line if transcript lines exist.

## Source trail

- Find an official source when possible.
- Add at least one source that supports scene identification.
- Add source notes explaining what each citation supports.
- Record `accessedAt` for outside links when useful.
- Add rights-holder and official-link information where possible.

## Editorial status checks

- `candidate`: lead exists, source may still be weak.
- `researching`: source hunt or factual confirmation is underway.
- `needs-transcription`: clip is chosen, transcript is not ready.
- `needs-native-review`: hearing or dialect call still needs a stronger pass.
- `ready-to-film`: archive argument is clear and materials are good enough to script around.
- `ready-to-publish`: metadata, packaging, sources, and rights trail are in place.

## Before publish

- Confirm `confidenceStatus`.
- Confirm `publishedAt` and `lastVerifiedAt`.
- Confirm SEO fields and poster copy.
- Confirm `coverTitle`, `shortCaption`, `hashtags`, `socialHook`, and CTA.
- Confirm the entry still works even if hosted media changes to `official-link-only` or `removed`.
